## Who This Is For

The ITU pledge formal metric is **27,079** (newsletter subscribers, Americas). That number lives on the official record and will be reported faithfully.

The real mandate is different.

**300 million people** across these 10 countries live in precarious informality. Street vendors. Day laborers. Domestic workers. Rural families. Artisans. Musicians. Informal entrepreneurs. People who navigate contracts, permits, government bureaucracy, and economic decisions without lawyers, accountants, or translators — alone, at night, on a $40 phone.

They are not a target market. They are not beneficiaries to be consulted. They are people. And the information that governs their lives — the clause in a contract that signs away their rights, the permit requirement they didn't know about, the certification that would triple their income — has been institutionally withheld from them. Not by malice. By architecture.

AKP is a gift. Not a product. Not a platform. A gift.

Zero cost to the end user. Always. No registration. No subscription. No terms of service written in a language they don't speak. The interface they already have: WhatsApp. The knowledge: theirs, in their language, on demand.

## The Delivery Architecture

The end user deploys nothing, pays nothing, registers for nothing.

```
TIER 1 — The People (300M+)
  Interface:  WhatsApp (the device they already have)
  Cost:       $0. Always.
  Skill:      None required.

TIER 2 — The Institutions That Serve Them
  (cooperatives · municipalities · cultural centers · IDB/CAF programs · NGOs)
  Interface:  Railway one-click deploy — this repository
  Cost:       $20–50/month serves 200–1,000 people
  Cost/user:  ~$0.04/month

TIER 3 — Developers and Builders
  Interface:  GitHub repo, open specification
  Cost:       Their time. Open license.
```

Avant.Dev sits at Tier 3. We build and give. Tier 2 deploys and serves. Tier 1 receives and uses.

## Why the AI Kernel Protocol Is a Policy Instrument

When a single jurisdiction can suspend frontier AI model access for all foreign nationals globally — without multilateral recourse, without due process, and without governance architecture designed to represent affected communities — the technology stack itself becomes a vector of dependence.

This happened. On June 12, 2026, frontier AI access was suspended for foreign nationals worldwide with 90 minutes notice. It was restored on June 30. The 300 million people this pledge serves had no voice in either decision.

Provider agnosticism is, in this context, a form of sovereignty. Not for developers. For the cooperative in Medellín that serves 500 artisans. For the municipal library in Kingston that offers document help. For the women's economic center in Guatemala City.

When their AI tool switches from Claude to Mistral to DeepSeek with one environment variable — because one government suspended one provider — they keep serving. That is what AKP is for.

## Addendum: Faith and Commitment to the Human Condition

This pledge began as a technical gesture. It became a question:

**What does a person earning $2 a day need from artificial intelligence?**

Not a dashboard. Not a consultation. Not to be named a beneficiary in someone else's grant application.

They need to understand the contract before they sign it. They need to know their rights before they lose them. They need to write the proposal that wins the contract. They need the answer to the bureaucracy question at 11pm, when the office is closed and they can't afford to take tomorrow off.

The information that governs their lives exists. It is in laws, in regulations, in contracts, in permit databases, in cultural heritage registries. It has always existed. What has not existed is a mechanism to make it available — in their language, at the moment of need, at zero cost, through the device in their pocket.

That is what AKP builds.

Not as a product. Not as a platform. Not as a service.

As a gift.

To every street vendor who signed a contract without understanding it. To every artisan who priced her work below its value because no one told her otherwise. To every musician who gave away rights he didn't know he had. To every family that missed a permit deadline because the form was in a language they don't read.

The pledge to the ITU is formal. The 27,079 is the number on the form.

The real number is 300 million. And the real pledge is simpler than any UN mandate:

**No one should lose what is theirs because they couldn't afford to understand it.**

This is what the AI Kernel Protocol is for.
